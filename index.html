Harmony @ Home

Play music effortlessly across multiple speakers without streaming.

If you have a home network, you may have tried sharing music between multiple devices using a utility such as AirFoil but AirFoil is expensive and dependent on streaming.

Harmony @ Home takes a different approach. Instead it plays identical music files it knows exists on all computers meaning that within a few seconds of synchronising all computers will have hours of synchronised music irrespective of network connectivity.

You're in control

Want to skip or pause a song? No problem Harmony @ Home notifies connected computers and resynchronises. Want to play a file that's not on all computers? Harmony pushes that file to all computers and then begins playing it as soon as transfer is complete.

Innovative at every level

Harmony @ Home uses IP addresses — still the most robust addressing mechanism for network communication — but displays your IP address as one or two words the moment you start the client. You only need to know one other computer to join a chain. The official client's minimalist interface puts the user experience first.

Free and multiplatform

Harmony @ Home will be initially available for Windows, Mac and Android with iOS to follow. The H@H protocol is open with ad-supported clients free and the official ad-free clients available for a small charge. In any chain one ad-free client removes ads on all clients.

FAQ

How much will the paid client cost?
Our aim is to keep clients below $5.00 US.
How is two words an IP address? Can I still use numbers?
Two words from a dictionary of 131,000 words can express any possible IPv4 address. You can still use the numbers if you prefer.
How does ad-removal work given the protocol is open?
The protocol allows for a challenge-response mechanism that the official clients use to determine if a paid client is part of the chain. While the protocol is open the challenge and responses aren't.
What about Bonjour and IPv6?
Support for both of these protocols are planned for future versions.
How is music matched on computers?
Music is matched based upon length and harmonic analysis of certain sample points. In this way, Harmony @ Home identifies identical music irrespective of the file format.
